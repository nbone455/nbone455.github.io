Music, Audio engineering

It’s easy to record good-sounding music (don’t listen to people who tell you otherwise!). I think everyone should at least try it, so I collected some basic suggestions and recommendations.

  • You can use basically free, open-source software to record good-sounding music: Audacity and Ardour

  • I use youtube-dl to download samples from youtube and remix them into beats/remixes

  • For recording acoustic instruments or voices, you’ll usually want a compressor microphone, which requires something called ‘phantom power’ (it’s just a small electrical charge). The best way to do this (cheaply) is to get something like this although there are plenty of other options.

  • W.I.P

Thinkers

Here are some philosophical thinkers that are interesting to me.

  • Massimo Pigliucci Yes, okay, he’s an evolutionary biologist but I am putting him here purely for his Stoicism stuff, and increasingly interesting Youtube channel

  • Joscha Bach AI researcher with a good reading on the pulse of AGI or artificial general intelligence. His philosophical stance addresses all of Western thought up until this point, which is impressive. Website is a bit outdated, this is a good place to start.

  • Bernardo Kastrup PhD in both Computer Science and Philosophy, one of the strongest (and only) modern-day proponents of Idealism specifically a form where there is nothing but mind. Great debater.

  • Peter Turchin an evolutionary biologist who tries to study how societies evolve. Lots of cool stuff on his website and books.

  • Robert LaFleur Cultural Anthropology & History, teaches at Beloit!, has a never-ending interpretation of the Lunar Calendar complete with translation from Chinese to English in addition to many interesting deep-dives on cultural phenomenon.

  • W.I.P
