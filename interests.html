Recording / Audio engineering

It’s easy to record good-sounding music (don’t listen to people who tell you otherwise!). I think everyone should at least try it, so I collected some basic suggestions and recommendations from my experience

  • You can use basically free, open-source software to record: Audacity and Ardour. There are lots of tutorials (like this), but you can usually figure out what you need from the manual + googling. If you’re not an open-source snob (or you’re loaded) you can use FL Studio or Ableton

  • I use youtube-dl to download samples from youtube and remix them into beats/remixes.

  • For recording acoustic instruments or vocals straight to your computer, you’llwant a compressor microphone which traditionally needs ‘phantom power’ from an audio-interface. The best way to do get around this (cheaply) is to get something like this mic although there are plenty of other options.

  • I skip the above by using an audio interface (Scarlett Solo) to plug my microphones/instruments into.

Thinkers (Living)

Here are some living philosophical thinkers that are interesting to me.

  • Joscha Bach AI researcher with a focus on AGI or artificial general intelligence. His philosophical stance (based around a computational explanation for consciousness) addresses a lot of Western philosophy up until this point in a scientific framework, which is impressive. Website is a bit outdated, this is a good place to start.

  • Bernardo Kastrup PhD in both Computer Science and Philosophy, one of the strongest (and only) modern-day proponents of Idealism. Very interesting guy, great debater, intellectually a ‘mirror’ to Bach (consciousness and thus reality as computation vs consciousness as reality).

  • Peter Turchin an evolutionary biologist who tries to study how societies evolve. Not sure he would consider himself a philosopher. Lots of cool stuff on his website and books.

  • David Chapman another AI researcher turned philosopher, attempts to solve the “meaning crisis” (coined by John Vervaeke – another interesting thinker). Very commendable (if not too dense) on-line book, 10/10 would recommend. Claims he “isn’t a philosopher”.

  • Donald Hoffman Cognitive scientist who studies visual systems and works on the consciousness ‘problem’. Uses evolutionary theory to hypothesize conscious subjects don’t see ‘real’ reality, just whatever gives the best fitness – like a virtual reality. Very interesting ideas that seem testable with evolutionary models.

  • Robert LaFleur Cultural Anthropology & History, teaches at Beloit!, has a never-ending interpretation of the Lunar Calendar complete with translation from Chinese to English in addition to many interesting deep-dives on cultural phenomenon.

Thinkers (not-living)

  • Erwin Schrödinger, specifically his lectures on the basis of life ‘What is Life?’ Famous quantum physicist, might know about the whole cat thing. Gave a series of lectures in 1944 on how life exists from first principles, and the resulting philosophy predicted a crystalline structure that transmits information through generations (hmm wonder what that could be).

  • Han-Shan (Cold Mountain) One of the many mountain recluses in the Chinese Buddhist/Daoist tradition, has some beautiful poems (some originally written on rocks, cliff-faces).

  • Diogenes a Greek philosopher who spent his days in a barrel. His only possession was a wooden bowl which he drank from. When he saw kids drinking directly from the river, he threw his bowl away. Founded the ’Cynic" philosophy, which is about being in harmony with nature/the state of things. Interesting guy.
