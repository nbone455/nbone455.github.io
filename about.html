About Nic Bone

Hello! I’m currently a PhD student in the Uyeda lab at Virginia Tech. I started in Fall 2019 (good timing eh?).

Before I was a PhD student I was an undergraduate at Beloit College, a tiny liberal arts college in Wisconsin. Before that, I was a nerd who liked birding (mainly hawks and owls), and trying to figure out how to port-forward so I could run my own Minecraft server. In terms of the amount of time I spent trouble-shooting, this was basically equivalent to being a PhD student in a computational lab…

My work now focuses on helping comparative biology answer more interesting questions by improving our methodology. This usually means making our computational methods match our biological assumptions and observations in order to get to the causes of macroevolutionary change. Basically, making our statistics answer the questions we actually want to answer.

For my dissertation I am attempting to do this for the problem of avian migration. I’ll talk about this more later!
